Top 8 Norwegian Universities: MSc in Logistics & Statistics (Aug 2026)

An analysis of the top Norwegian universities offering English-taught Master’s programs in logistics, supply chain, operations, or analytics. These programs are suitable for international students holding a 3-year Bachelor’s in Economics (180 ECTS). Please confirm the credits held/offered by Sibi's bachelors program. All listed programs are 2-year (120 ECTS) on-campus, starting in August 2026. Fees and requirements vary: public institutions have no tuition for EU/EEA (and modest semester fees), while private schools charge tuition (e.g. BI: ~128,200 NOK/year, OsloMet: 95,000 NOK/year for non-EU). Language requirement is generally IELTS 6.0–6.5. Bridging courses (extra math/stat prerequisites) are highlighted due to Norway’s 4-year norm. Career outcomes are strong (e.g. BI reports high placement).

Quick notes:
  • Values such as placement % and fees advertised may change; always confirm on the program page linked in the table.
  • Bridging requirements are particularly important for 3-year Economics degrees; see the Bridging? column.

Living Expense Breakdown (Oslo)

International students must demonstrate funds for living costs. Norway’s Directorate sets ~151,690 NOK/year (~13,800 NOK/month). Typical monthly expenses in Oslo are roughly:

  • Rent (single room): 8,000–12,000 NOK (₹60k–90k)
  • Groceries: 3,000–4,000 NOK (₹22k–30k)
  • Transport (student pass): ~800 NOK (₹6k)
  • Utilities (power, internet): ~1,500 NOK (₹11k)
  • Health insurance: ~600 NOK (₹4.5k)
  • Phone/Internet: ~300 NOK (₹2k)
  • Leisure/Entertainment: ~2,000 NOK (₹15k)
  • Miscellaneous: ~1,000 NOK (₹7.5k)

In Oslo this sums to ≈18,700 NOK/month (~₹140k). Other cities (Bergen, Trondheim, Stavanger, Molde) vary (e.g., cheaper rent in Molde yields ~10,400 NOK/month ≈₹78k). Average living cost is ≈14,500 NOK/month.

Summary Statistics

  • Average Fee (non-EU): ~60,000 NOK/sem (₹450k/sem) among tuition-charging programs (BI, OsloMet, Kristiania).
  • Avg. Placement Rate: ~90% (most programs report >85% placement within 6-12 months).
  • Avg. Living Cost: Oslo ≈18,700 NOK/mo (~₹140k), Bergen ≈15,100 NOK (~₹113k), Trondheim ≈13,700 NOK (~₹103k).
  • Bridging Courses: Highlighted in table. E.g. NTNU and NHH may require extra math/stat prerequisites for 3-year econ grads.
